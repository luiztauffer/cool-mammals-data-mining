 p  The Asiatic lion is smaller than the African and has a distinctive fold of skin on the belly. Also, the Asiatic male's mane is smaller and lighter in colour. Lions spend most of their time in a social group called the 'pride', which is made up of adult females, their offspring and adult males. Both sexes defend their territory by roaring, urine marking and patrolling.  p 
 p 2 - 4 cubs are born after a gestation of 112 days (3.5 months).  p 
 p The main threats are hunting and habitat destruction.  p 
 p Less than 300 Asiatic lions survive in the wild in the Gir National Park and Lion Sanctuary (India). There are conservation breeding programmes in zoos including a European Endangered species Programme (EEP).  p
