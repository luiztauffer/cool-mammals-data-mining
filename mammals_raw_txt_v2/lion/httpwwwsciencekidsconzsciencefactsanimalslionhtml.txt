 p Lions are the second largest big cat species in the world  (behind tigers).  p 
 p The average male lion weighs around 180 kg  (400 lb) while the average female lion weighs around 130 kg  (290 lb).  p 
 p The heaviest lion on record weighed an amazing 375 kg  (826 lb).  p 
 p Lions can reach speeds of up to 81 kph (50 mph)  but only in short bursts because of a lack of stamina.  p 
 p The roar of a lion can be heard from 8 kilometers  (5.0 miles) away.  p 
 p Most lions found in the wild live in southern and eastern  parts of Africa.  p 
 p Lions are very social compared to other cat species, often  living in prides that feature females, offspring and a few adult males.  p 
 p Male lions are easy to recognize thanks to their distinctive  manes. Males with darker manes are more likely to attract female lions  (lionesses).  p 
 p Lions are the national animal of Albania,  Belgium, Bulgaria,  England, Ethiopia,  Luxembourg, the  Netherlands and  Singapore.  p 
 p Lions in the wild live for around 12 years.  p 
 p When lions breed with tigers the resulting hybrids are known  as ligers and tigons. There are also lion and leopard hybrids known as leopons  and lion and jaguar hybrids known as jaglions.  p 
 p Lionesses are better hunters than males and do most of the  hunting for a pride.  p 
 p In the wild, lions rest for around 20 hours a day.  p
