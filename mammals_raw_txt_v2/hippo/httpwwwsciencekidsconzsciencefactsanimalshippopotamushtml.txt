 p Hippopotamuses  are found in Africa.  p 
 p The name  hippopotamus means ‘river horse’ and is often shortened to hippo.  p 
 p The  hippopotamus is generally considered the third largest land mammal (after the White  rhinoceros and elephant).  p 
 p Hippopotamuses  spend a large amount of time in water such as rivers, lakes and swamps.  p 
 p Resting in  water helps keep hippopotamuses temperature down.  p 
 p Hippopotamuses  give birth in water.  p 
 p Hippopotamuses  have short legs, a huge mouth and a body shaped like a barrel.  p 
 p The closest  relations of the hippopotamus are surprisingly cetaceans such as whales and  dolphins.  p 
 p Scientists  believe this family of animals diverged in evolution around 55 million years  ago.  p 
 p Although  hippos might look a little chubby, they can easily outrun a human.  p 
 p Hippos can  be extremely aggressive, especially if they feel threatened.  p 
 p They are  regarded as one of the most dangerous animals in Africa.  p 
 p Hippos are  threatened by habitat loss and poachers who hunt them for their meat and teeth.  p 
 p A male  hippopotamus is called a ‘bull’.  p 
 p A female  hippopotamus is called a ‘cow’.  p 
 p A baby hippo  is called a ‘calf’.  p 
 p A group of  hippos in known as a ‘herd’, ‘pod’, ‘dale’ or ‘bloat’.  p 
 p Hippos  typically live for around 45 years.  p 
 p Hippos eat mostly grass.   p
