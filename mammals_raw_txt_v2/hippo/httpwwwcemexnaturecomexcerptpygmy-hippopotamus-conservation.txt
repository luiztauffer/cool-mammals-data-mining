 p style="text-align: justify;" The  a href="https:  en.wikipedia.org wiki Pygmy_hippopotamus" target="_blank" pygmy hippopotamus  a  is considerably less common than its larger and more famous relative, the  a href="https:  en.wikipedia.org wiki Hippopotamidae" target="_blank" hippopotamus  a . Pygmy hippos live in Liberia, Ivory Coast, Guinea and Sierra Leone, in  a href="https:  en.wikipedia.org wiki West_Africa" target="_blank" West Africa  a .  p 
 p style="text-align: justify;" Over the past century, the pygmy hippo’s habitat has declined dramatically as a result of logging, farming and human settlement. As deforestation continues and the animal’s habitat becomes more fragmented, available populations are coming under increasing pressure.  p 
 p style="text-align: justify;" Although conservation initiatives are ongoing, without more information on the species’ status and a coordinated conservation strategy, the pygmy hippo may disappear from the wild. At the present, there is a strong captive population, which has doubled in size over the past 25 years, potentially providing a safeguard for this species.  p
