 p But with some of nature’s most incredible spectacles on the country’s doorstep, there are a few  strong Colombian animals  strong  that will always make it worth your while. Don’t miss these: b  span style="text-decoration: underline"  br

 p Colombia is one of the best places in the world to see  strong humpback whales  strong , which migrate to the warm waters off the country’s  strong Pacific coast  strong  every year to mate and give birth. July, August and September are the best months to see these incredible beasts heave themselves out of the water and enjoy the rugged beauty of this region.  p 
 p Photo:  a href="https:  upload.wikimedia.org wikipedia commons 7 71 Choloepus_hoffmanni_(Puerto_Viejo,_CR)_crop.jpg" target="_blank" Wikipedia  a   p 
 p Sloths are probably the most loveable creature in Colombia, famous for their three distinctive toes and the fact they move so slowly. Sloths never look agitated or upset and it’s worth travelling all the way to  strong Colombia’s Amazon rainforest  strong  just to see these gentle leaf-eaters in their natural habitat, high up in the trees. b    b   p 
 p Photo:  a href="https:  www.flickr.com photos 31830413@N06 2977317822" target="_blank" R Ruiz.  a   p 
 p River dolphins are famous in Amazon folklore for they are said to turn into handsome young men at night, seducing the local girls and making them pregnant. And these  strong freshwater dolphins  strong  are worth taking a boat trip on the  strong Amazon  strong  to see. They’re huge –  up to 2.5m (8.2 ft) – and are either light grey or carnation pink.  p 
 p Photo:  a href="https:  upload.wikimedia.org wikipedia commons 0 0c Tortue_imbriqueeld4.jpg" target="_blank" Wikipedia  a   p 
 p Colombia has a way with giant water beasts and watching the country’s enormous  strong sea turtles  strong  crawl onto the beach to lay their eggs is a sight you’ll never forget. Leatherback turtles can live for up to 100 years, laying their eggs annually on some of Colombia’s least known  strong Caribbean beaches  strong . February to July are the best months to see them.  p 
 p Photo:  a href="https:  upload.wikimedia.org wikipedia commons 5 53 Carcharinus_galapagensis_1.jpg" target="_blank" Wikipedia  a   p 
 p Colombia is home to sharks’ paradise – the small, uninhabited island of  strong Malpelo  strong , around 500km (310 miles) west of  strong Colombia’s Pacific coast  strong . Its waters are home to a unique population of sharks, including hammerheads, silky sharks and the rare smalltooth sand tiger. If diving is your thing, don’t miss the chance to see them.  p 
 p Photo:  a href="https:  upload.wikimedia.org wikipedia commons 1 1f Grapsus_grapsus_Galapagos_Islands.jpg" target="_blank" Wikipedia  a   p 
 p Once a year, for just two weeks, heavily pregnant black land crabs emerge from their holes on Providencia Island and head to the sea to deposit their eggs. When their tiny offspring hatch, they migrate back up the beach. This incredible sight has become so precious, the migrating crabs now have their own military protection.  p
