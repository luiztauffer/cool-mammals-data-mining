 p The tiger is the biggest species of the cat family.  p 
 p Tigers can reach a length of up to 3.3 metres (11 feet) and  weigh as much as 300 kilograms (660 pounds).  p 
 p Subspecies of the tiger include the Sumatran Tiger, Siberian  Tiger, Bengal Tiger, South China Tiger, Malayan Tiger and Indochinese Tiger.  p 
 p Many subspecies of the tiger are either endangered or  already extinct. Humans are the primary cause of this through hunting and the  destruction of habitats.  p 
 p Around half of tiger cubs don’t live beyond two years of  age.  p 
 p Tiger cubs leave their mother when they are around 2 years  of age.  p 
 p A group of tigers is known as an ‘ambush’ or ‘streak’.  p 
 p Tigers are good swimmers and can swim up to 6 kilometres.  p 
 p Rare white tigers carry a gene that is only present in  around 1 in every 10000 tigers.  p 
 p Tigers usually hunt alone at night time.  p 
 p Tigers have been known to reach speeds up to 65 kph  (40 mph).  p 
 p Less than 10% of hunts end successfully for tigers  p 
 p Tigers can easily jump over 5 metres in length.  p 
 p Various tiger subspecies are the national animals of Bangladesh,  India, North    Korea, South    Korea and Malaysia.  p 
 p There are more tigers held privately as pets than there are  in the wild.  p 
 p Tigers that breed with lions give birth to hybrids known as tigons and  ligers.  p
