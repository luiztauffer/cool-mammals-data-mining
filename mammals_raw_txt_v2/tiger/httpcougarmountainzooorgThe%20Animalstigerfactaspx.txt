 p class="MsoNormal" 
        This WORLD is represented by the magnificent Bengal Tiger, the only one of the 
        six existing tiger subspecies that occurs in different color variations in the 
        wild.  p 
 p class="MsoNormal" 
         b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal" Common Name: span style="mso-tab-count:1"         Bengal 
        Tiger  span   b   p 
 p class="MsoNormal" 
         b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal" Genus Species: span style="mso-tab-count:1; font-style: italic;"          Panthera tigris  span   b   p 
 p class="MsoNormal" 
         b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal" Range: p   p   b   p 
 p class="MsoNormal" 
        The Bengal Tiger is found in parts of India, Bangladesh, Nepal, Bhutan and 
        Burma.  p 
 p class="MsoNormal" 
         b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal" Natural Habitat: p   p   b   p 
 p class="MsoNormal" 
        The habitat is varied: span style="mso-spacerun:yes"     span grasslands, 
        subtropical and tropical rainforests, scrub forests, wet and dry deciduous 
        forests and mangroves.  p 
 p class="MsoNormal" 
         b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal" Physical Appearance: p   p   b   p 
 p class="MsoNormal" 
        Physical appearance may slightly vary depending on geographic area. The Bengal Tiger is the second largest of all living tiger subspecies. span style="mso-spacerun:yes"     span The male tigers can weigh from 
        450-550 lbs and the females can get up to 310 lbs. span style="mso-spacerun:yes"     span they have orange coats, a fair 
        (whitish) medial and ventral area and stripes that can vary in color from brown 
        to black. span style="mso-spacerun:yes"     span The form and density of 
        stripes differs between subspecies, but most tigers have in excess of 100 
        stripes. span style="mso-spacerun:yes"     span Each striping pattern is 
        unique to each animal, acting the same way as fingerprints do for humans.  p 
 p class="MsoNormal" 
         b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal" Reproduction: p   p   b   p 
 p class="MsoNormal" 
        Male and female tigers breed often and noisily. span style="mso-spacerun:yes"  
          span The female is only receptive for a couple of days so frequent mating is 
        preferred (every 15-20 minutes for 5-6 days). span style="mso-spacerun:yes"  
          span A female estrus lasts 5-7 days and occurs approximately every 30 days. span style="mso-spacerun:yes"     span Gestation period is approximately 103 
        days and litters of 2 to 4 are common. span style="mso-spacerun:yes"  
          span Cubs are approximately 2 lbs when they are born. span style="mso-spacerun:yes"     span The cubs become independent around 18 
        months of age and leave their mother between 2 to 2-1 2 years of age. span style="mso-spacerun:yes"     span Female tigers become sexually mature 
        at around 3 years of age whereas male are not sexually mature until they are 4-5 
        years of age.  p 
 p class="MsoNormal" 
         b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal" STATUS in Natural Habitat: p   p   b   p 
 p class="MsoNormal" 
        Critically Endangered. span style="mso-spacerun:yes"     span Almost 
        certain to become extinct within 10 years due to over poaching and habitat 
        destruction if conservation methods are not successful.  p 
 p class="MsoNormal" 
         b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal" Cool Fact:  b   p 
 p class="MsoNormal" 
        Tigers are excellent and strong swimmers. They enjoy lying in rivers, lakes and 
        ponds and have been known to hunt and kill in the water.  p
