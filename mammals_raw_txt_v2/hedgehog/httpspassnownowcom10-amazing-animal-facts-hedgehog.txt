 p style="text-align: justify;" The hedgehog is a short and stout little mammal. Most mammals have fur or hair that is somewhat flexible and soft. But the hair on the back of a hedgehog is a thick layer of spikes (or modified hairs) known as quills. These quills are made of keratin, the same stuff our hair and fingernails are made of.  p 
 p style="text-align: justify;"  span style="color: #ff0000;"  strong Fast Facts about Hedgehogs  strong   span   p 
 p style="text-align: justify;" 1. There are 17 species of hedgehog.  p 
 p style="text-align: justify;" 2. The hedgehog is nocturnal, coming out at night and spending the day sleeping in a nest under bushes or thick shrubs.  p 
 p style="text-align: justify;" 3. They have about 5000 spines.  Each spine lasts about a year then drops out and a replacement grows.  p 
 p style="text-align: justify;" 4. The spines of a hedgehogs, are stiff hollow hairs, they are not poisonous or barbed and cannot be easily removed.  p 
 p style="text-align: justify;" 5. Most hedgehog species will roll into a tight ball if threatened, making it hard for its attacker to get past the spiky defenses.  p 
 p style="text-align: justify;" 6. A baby hedgehog is called a hoglet.  p 
 p style="text-align: justify;" 7. Hedgehogs communicate through a combination of snuffles, grunts and squeals.  p 
 p style="text-align: justify;" 8. These cute animals eat almost any insect, including worms, centipedes,snails, frogs, mice and even snakes.  p 
 p style="text-align: justify;" 9. Hedgehogs hibernate in the winter in cold areas. In the desert, they burrow under the ground during the day to stay cool.  p 
 p style="text-align: justify;" 10. For their size hedgehogs have a relatively long lifespan. They live on average for 4 – 7 years in the wild and longer in captivity.  p
