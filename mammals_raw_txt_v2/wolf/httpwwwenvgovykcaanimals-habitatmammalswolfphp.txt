Though some wolves are solitary, they generally form  packs. The basic social unit of a wolf pack is the mated pair, accompanied by  the pair's adult offspring. The mated pair form the alphas and are dominant in  the social order. They are the only members allowed to breed in the pack. The  average pack size in   span  span style="font-family:verdana;font-size:12px;" Yukon  span  span style="font-family:verdana;font-size:12px;"  is seven to nine wolves, though  smaller and larger pack sizes are not uncommon.  Formation of a pack is very important for  co-operative hunting and raising pups.    span   span   p
