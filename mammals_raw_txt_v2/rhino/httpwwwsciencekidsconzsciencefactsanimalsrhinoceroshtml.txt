 p The name  rhinoceros means ‘nose horn’ and is often shortened to rhino.  p 
 p There are  five different species of rhinoceros, three native to southern Asia and two  native to Africa. They are the Black Rhinoceros, White Rhinoceros, Indian  Rhinoceros, Javan Rhinoceros and Sumatran Rhinoceros.  p 
 p All five  species of rhinoceros can grow to weigh over 1000 kg (2200 lb).  p 
 p White rhino  can weigh over 3500 kg (7700 lb).  p 
 p White rhinoceros  are generally considered the second largest land mammal (after the elephant).  p 
 p Three of the  five rhinoceros species are listed as being critically endangered.  p 
 p Rhinoceros  have thick, protective skin.  p 
 p Relative to  their large body size, rhinoceros have small brains.  p 
 p Rhinoceros  horns are made from a protein called keratin, the same substance that  fingernails and hair are made of.  p 
 p Rhinoceros  are often hunted by humans for their horns.  p 
 p Rhinoceros  are herbivores (plant eaters).  p 
 p A group of  rhinoceros is called a ‘herd’ or a ‘crash’.  p 
 p Despite their name, White Rhinoceros are  actually gray.   p
