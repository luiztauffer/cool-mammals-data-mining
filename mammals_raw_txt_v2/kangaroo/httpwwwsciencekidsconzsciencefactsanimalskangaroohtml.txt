 p Kangaroos  are marsupial animals that are found in Australia as well as New Guinea.  p 
 p There are  four different kangaroo species, the red kangaroo, eastern grey kangaroo,  western grey kangaroo and antilopine kangaroo.  p 
 p Kangaroos  can hop around quickly on two legs or walk around slowly on all four.  p 
 p Kangaroos  can’t walk backwards.  p 
 p Kangaroos  have very powerful legs and can be dangerous at times.  p 
 p Kangaroos  can jump very high, sometimes three times their own height.  p 
 p Kangaroos  can swim.  p 
 p Most  kangaroos eat grass.  p 
 p Baby  kangaroos are known as ‘joeys’.  p 
 p A group of  kangaroos is called a ‘mob’, ‘troop’ or ‘court’.  p 
 p The red  kangaroo is the largest marsupial in the world.  p 
 p Kangaroos  usually live to around six years old in the wild.  p 
 p Australian airline Qantas uses a kangaroo as  their symbol.   p
