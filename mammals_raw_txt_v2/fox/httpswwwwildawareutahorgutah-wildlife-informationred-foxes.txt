 p The red fox  em Vulpes vulpes  em  has a wide distribution throughout North America, Europe, and Asia. Red fox are common in Utah. They are found in open and semi-open habitats, although they occasionally occur in heavily forested areas, cities, and suburban settings.  p 
 p The red fox is a member of the canid family, which includes wolves,  a href="https:  www.wildawareutah.org utah-wildlife-information coyotes " coyotes  a , and domestic dogs. Contrary to its name, the red fox is not always red, but can be any shade between red and black. The red fox can be distinguished from other fox species by the characteristic white tip on the end of the tail.  p 
 p Foxes are opportunistic omnivores and skilled scavengers. They feed mostly on small mammals, insects, amphibians, fish, fruits, berries, nuts, eggs and carrion.  p 
 p Red fox may be active any time, day or night, but are most active at dawn and dusk. Screeching yowls, barking, yipping and howling are common vocalizations. Fox also have a very strong musk odor.  p 
 p The breeding season occurs from mid-January to early February. Red fox usually have a single annual litter of 4-5 kits. Fox kits, or pups, are born from March to May in dens dug in the ground or under rocks or structures. Red fox may dig their own dens or use abandoned burrows. They usually have one primary den for their young, called a natal den, which may be used year after year. The young begin to hunt with the adults at about 8-12 weeks old. They stay with their parents through the spring and summer months and disperse to find their own territories by the fall.  p
