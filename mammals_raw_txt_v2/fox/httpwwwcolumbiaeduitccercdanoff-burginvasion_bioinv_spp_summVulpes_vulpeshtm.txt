 p style="margin-right:.5in;margin-left:.5in"  b  span style="font-size:10.0pt;

normal" Vulpes  i   i style="mso-bidi-font-style:normal" vulpes  i  is an
extraordinary adaptor. span style="mso-spacerun: yes"     span Although
primarily a predator carnivore it is also capable of omnivorous behavior,
allowing it to forage successfully when prey is scarce. span style="mso-spacerun: yes"     span This also aids it’s survival in areas

sustenance. span style="mso-spacerun: yes"     span It is a clever adaptor
to habitat changes, and can survive thrive in all but the most extreme dessert
or tropical habitats. span style="mso-spacerun: yes"     span The presence
of dingoes in a habitat may be one of the few non-climate controlled factors to
limit the Red Foxes growth a name="Ecology"   a . span style="mso-spacerun:

predator. span style="mso-spacerun: yes"     span It may in fact be a
keystone species in areas where it is distributed naturally, keeping the number
of rodents and small mammals in check. span style="mso-spacerun: yes"

recent years.  p   p   span   p 
 p style="margin-right:.5in;margin-left:.5in"  a name="Benefits"   a  b  span style="font-size:10.0pt;font-family:Arial" Benefit(s): span style="mso-spacerun: yes"     span   span   b  span style="font-size:10.0pt;
font-family:Arial" As noted above the Red Fox can provide benefits to ranchers,
farmers, and urban dwellers by keeping the number of rodents in check.  span  span style="font-size:10.0pt;mso-bidi-font-size:12.0pt;font-family:Arial"  It may
also provide balance from an ecological point of view, as a keystone predator
for some of its natural range. p   p   span   p 
 p style="margin-right:.5in;margin-left:.5in"  a name="Threats"   a  b  span style="font-size:10.0pt;font-family:Arial" Threat(s):  span   b  span style="font-size:10.0pt;mso-bidi-font-size:12.0pt;font-family:Arial"  span style="mso-spacerun: yes"      span The Red Fox as an introduced
species (Australia) is devastating to the native wildlife. span style="mso-spacerun: yes"     span The fox may be solely responsible for
dozens of small mammal extinctions in Australia. span style="mso-spacerun:
yes"     span Several species of endangered ground nesting birds, small
mammals, amphibians, and reptiles are currently threatened by the fox’s
presence in Australia. span style="mso-spacerun: yes"     span The Red Fox
also contributes to the spread of disease, due to the widespread nature of it’s
range and its resistance to population control methods, it could additionally
be a key carrier of rabies, were that disease to ever be introduced to
Australia. span style="mso-spacerun: yes"     span The Red Fox also
threatens local livestock due to predation. p   p   span   p 
 p style="margin-right:.5in;margin-left:.5in"  a name="Control"   a  b  span style="font-size:10.0pt;font-family:Arial" Control Level Diagnosis:  span   b  span style="font-size:10.0pt;mso-bidi-font-size:12.0pt;font-family:Arial"  span style="mso-spacerun: yes"     span The outlook for the control of this
pest in Australia is not bright. span style="mso-spacerun: yes"  
  span Due to its adaptability, it has become firmly entrenched throughout the
main landmass of Australia. span style="mso-spacerun: yes"     span There
have even been possible sightings in the island of Tasmania, which has thus far
escaped invasion, much to the benefit of its unique ecology. span style="mso-spacerun: yes"     span Most control methods (see below for
specific details) are expensive and not effective on a wide scale. span style="mso-spacerun: yes"     span However the need for immediate,
successful control measures has probably never been greater, with the numbers
of endangered species currently threatened by the fox steadily decreasing, as
well as the potential for a rabies infected animal introduction increasing
daily. p   p   span   p 
 p style="margin-right:.5in;margin-left:.5in"  b  span style="font-size:10.0pt;
font-family:Arial" Control Method:  span   b  span style="font-size:10.0pt;
mso-bidi-font-size:12.0pt;font-family:Arial"  span style="mso-spacerun:
yes"     span There are some substantive control mechanisms currently
being used, however they are expensive and not terribly successful on a wide
scale measurement. span style="mso-spacerun: yes"     span The use of the
poison 1080 in fox “baiting” is used in some areas of Australia where the
poison will not affect the native wildlife. span style="mso-spacerun:
yes"     span Immunocontraception is a method currently being tested that
introduces a chemical that renders the foxes infertile, although this is
proceeding slowly due to the recency of this solution, the need for further
research, and lack of data that indicates how many foxes must be infertile to
control the population. span style="mso-spacerun: yes"     span Offering a
bounty on foxes has also been explored, although has not proven effective in
reducing or controlling the Red Fox numbers due to its expense and inability to
target fit foxes for removal. span style="mso-spacerun: yes"  
  span Finally, the fur trade was once a method of control but reduced demand
has caused this solution to fall away as well. p   p   span   p
