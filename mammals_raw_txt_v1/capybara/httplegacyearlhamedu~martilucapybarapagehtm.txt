        name Hydrochaeris literally means "water pig"  font   b   font   p 
 p align="center"  font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"  font size="-1"  b Introduction  b   font   font   p 
 p  font size="-1" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" color="#339933"  b  font color="#FF8000" Physical 
        Characteristics:   font  Capybaras are the largest rodents in the world 
        and are semi-aquatic. They grow to be 3-4 feet long and can weigh 60-170 
        pounds. They stand about 1.5 feet high. Capybaras have no tail and short 
        legs with webbed feet. Their front feet have 4 toes each and the back 
        feet have 3 toes. Their fur is semi-long and coarse with color ranging 
        from brown, gray, yellow and black. They have an approximate lifespan 
        of 10 years in the wild and up to 12 years in captivity.  b   font   p 
 p  font size="-1" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" color="#339933"  b  font color="#FF8000" Lifestyle: 
          font Their natural habitat is swamps, marshes, and forests in Central 
        and South America; including the Amazon rainforest, Panama, Columbia, 
        Uruguay, and Argentina. They are herbivores with a diet consisting of 
        water plants, grains, fruits, and grasses. Like other rodents they need 
        to gnaw and chew in order to wear their two front teeth down, which grow 
        through out their lives.   b   font   p 
 p  font size="-1" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" color="#339933"  b  font color="#FF8000" Behavior: 
          font They are active during the morning and night, and rest during the 
        day in burrows. Capybaras are social animals gathering in groups of around 
        20 individuals. They communicate using whistles, barks, grunts, squeaks 
        and clicks. They also have a sent producing gland in their neck that is 
        thought to be used for marking territory and identification.   b   font   p 
 p  font size="-1" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" color="#339933"  b  font color="#FF8000" Predators: 
          font The Capybaras predators are the puma, jaguar, caiman, ocelot, harpy 
        eagle, anaconda and people. When threatened they scurry to the water and 
        submerge themselves with only their eyes and nose exposed (see picture 
        to upper left).  b   font   p 
 p  font size="-1" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" color="#339933"  b  font color="#FF8000" Sexual 
        Reproduction:  font  The capybara reaches sexual maturity between 15 and 
        18 months. Their breading season peaks in April and May, and they have 
        a gestation period of 149-156 days. They give birth to one litter a year 
        with up to 8 babies per litter and an average of 4. Newborn capybaras 
        weigh around 2 pounds and is already equipped with hair and sight.   b   font   p

 p align="center"  font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="-1"  b Conservation 
        Status  b   font   p 
 p align="left"  font size="-1" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"  b  font color="#339933"  
        The Capybara is not an endangered species. It in neither on the IUCN list 
        ( a href="http:  www.redlist.org" www.redlist.org  a )or the CITES list. 
        ( a href="http:  www.cites.org eng resources fauna.shtml" www.cites.org eng resources fauna.shtml  a ) 
          font   b   font   p 
 p align="center"  font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"  b  font size="-1" Conservation 
        Issues   font   b   font   p 
 p align="left"  font color="#008000"  b  font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="-1" color="#339933" The 
        biggest threat to the capybara is the loss of it's rainforest habitat. 
        Because of logging, farming and the clear-cutting burning techniques that 
        are being used, their habitat is being diminished every day. 33.8 million 
        acres of rainforest are lost each year, 2.8 million acres a month, 93,000 
        acres a day, 3,800 acres an hour and 64 acres a minute. Brazil alone lost 
        91.4 million acres fo tropical rainforest between 1980 and 1990. Because 
        around 50 percent of the earths species live in tropical rainforests, 
        this massive loss of their habitat causes the extinction of aproximatly 
        100 species a day. Luckily for the Capybara and other tropical rainforest 
        dwellers the country of Suriname, with the help of Conservation International 
        and other conservation groups, has created one of the largest nature reserves 
        in the world on the Atlantic coast of South America. It's area is 1.592 
        million hectares. The habitat of the capybara is protected in the reserve, 
        which is mostly tropical rainforest. The capybara is also common in North 
        American zoos which ensures it survival even if it is not in its natural 
        habitat.   font   b   font   p
