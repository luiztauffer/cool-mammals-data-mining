 p style="text-align: justify;" 
	In our study, we aimed to identify and quantify the intestinal ciliates in a Brazilian capybara and to compare the obtained data with previous studies on the capybara ciliate community in other geographic locations within the American continent. We identified 20 species belonging to four families and ten genera. This is the first study on intestinal ciliates in Brazilian capybara since the last reports for the country in the 1960s. Among the identified species,  em Anacharon gracilis  em ,  em A. lepturus  em ,  em Cycloposthium bursa  em ,  em Monoposthium cynodontum  em ,  em Ogimotopsis pumila  em ,  em Paracunhamunizia calocoma  em ,  em Protohallia nana  em  and  em Uropogon urai  em  were recorded for the first time in Brazil, and the giant ciliate  em Muniziella cunhai  em  was observed for the second time in a symbiotic association with capybaras in the country. The present study highlighted the importance of knowing the gastrointestinal ciliate community associated with wild hosts in order to better understand their geographic distribution and host specificity.  p
