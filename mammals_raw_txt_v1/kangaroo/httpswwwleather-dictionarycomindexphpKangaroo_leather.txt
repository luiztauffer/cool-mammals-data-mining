Kangaroo leather is considered an  a href=" index.php Exotic_leather" title="Exotic leather" exotic leather  a  in many countries. However, in Australia, where the animals exist naturally and in abundance, the leather is as "normal" as  a href=" index.php Deerskin" title="Deerskin" deerskin  a  in northern Europe and northern America. Kangaroos are not bred. Due to a lack of enemies in their natural habitat, their numbers have increased,leading to them being hunted. In Australia, kangaroo meat is heavily processed into dog food. However, a large part of the meat is also exported and is popular in other countries since the BSE crisis (aka mad cow disease) and also because of its low fat content.

 p Kangaroo leather is considered to be very  a href=" index.php Leather_quality" title="Leather quality" tear resistant  a  and can be thinned and made lighter than  a href=" index.php Cow_leather" title="Cow leather" cow leather  a  without affecting its stability. The average  a href=" index.php Thickness_of_leather" title="Thickness of leather" thickness  a  is 1 millimetre, which is less than  a href=" index.php Cow_leather" title="Cow leather" cattle leather  a  which is, 1.2 mm to 1.4 mm thick. However, it is almost twice as strong. These properties are due to very strong cross links of the skin fibre.

They are usually  a href=" index.php Chrome_tanned" title="Chrome tanned" chromium-tanned  a  and do not look very different from  a href=" index.php Cow_leather" title="Cow leather" cow leather  a .
