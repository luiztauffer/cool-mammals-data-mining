 p  span style="FONT-SIZE: 14pt; FONT-FAMILY: 'Times New Roman'; FONT-WEIGHT: bold; COLOR: #0080c0" Can I bring any meat, poultry or pork products into the U.S.?  span   p 
 p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN-BOTTOM: 12pt; BACKGROUND: white; LINE-HEIGHT: 18pt"  b  u style="FONT-SIZE: 12pt; FONT-FAMILY: Gill Sans MT; FONT-WEIGHT: bold; COLOR: #000000" Animal Products and Animal By-Products  u   b   p 
 p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN-BOTTOM: 12pt; FONT-SIZE: 12pt; FONT-FAMILY: Gill Sans MT; COLOR: #000000; LINE-HEIGHT: 18pt; mso-margin-top-alt: auto"  span style="FONT-SIZE: 12pt; FONT-FAMILY: Gill Sans MT; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; COLOR: #000000; mso-bidi-font-weight: bold" There are restrictions on the animal products that travelers may bring into the United States. All decisions about the admissibility of animal products are dependent on disease conditions in their country of origin or the country where the products were processed and or packaged. Because disease conditions can change at a moment's notice, travelers who purchase such goods must be prepared for the fact that the goods may be confiscated during customs clearance.  span   p 
 p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN-BOTTOM: 12pt; BACKGROUND: white; LINE-HEIGHT: 18pt"  span style="FONT-SIZE: 12pt; FONT-FAMILY: Gill Sans MT; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; COLOR: #000000; mso-bidi-font-weight: bold"  span style="FONT-SIZE: 12pt; FONT-FAMILY: Gill Sans MT; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; COLOR: #000000; mso-bidi-font-weight: bold" The importation of fresh, dried or canned meats or meat products is generally not allowed from most foreign countries into the United States. This includes products that have been prepared with meat.  span   span   p 
 p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN-BOTTOM: 12pt; FONT-SIZE: 12pt; FONT-FAMILY: Gill Sans MT; BACKGROUND: white; LINE-HEIGHT: 18pt"  span style="COLOR: #000000" Beef and beef products from a country not known to be infected with  span   a style="TEXT-DECORATION: underline; COLOR: #0000ff" href="https:  www.fda.gov AnimalVeterinary GuidanceComplianceEnforcement ComplianceEnforcement BovineSpongiformEncephalopathy ucm2006517.htm" Bovine Spongiform Encephalopathy  a   span style="COLOR: #000000" (BSE) and  span   a style="TEXT-DECORATION: underline; COLOR: #0000ff" href="https:  www.aphis.usda.gov publications animal_health 2013 fs_fmd_general.pdf" Foot-and-Mouth Disease  a   span style="COLOR: #000000" (FMD) generally are enterable. However, like all other  em  b  span style="FONT-WEIGHT: normal; FONT-STYLE: normal" food  span   b   em  items the meat must be unopened and commercially label indicating the country of origin and meat type.  span   p 
 p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN-BOTTOM: 12pt; FONT-SIZE: 12pt; FONT-FAMILY: Gill Sans MT; BACKGROUND: white; LINE-HEIGHT: 18pt"  span style="FONT-SIZE: 12pt; FONT-FAMILY: Gill Sans MT; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; COLOR: #000000; mso-bidi-font-weight: bold"  span style="FONT-FAMILY: Gill Sans MT; COLOR: #000000"  span style="mso-bidi-font-weight: bold" The regulations on importing meat and meat products change frequently because they are based on disease outbreaks in different areas of the world.  span  The best source for the current disease span style="COLOR: #000000" s  span  can be found on starting on  a style="TEXT-DECORATION: underline; COLOR: #0000ff" href="https:  usdasearch.usda.gov search?affiliate=usda-aphis&amp;query=current+disease+status" APHIS  a  website.  span   span   p 
 p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN-BOTTOM: 12pt; FONT-SIZE: 12pt; FONT-FAMILY: Gill Sans MT; BACKGROUND: white; COLOR: #000000; LINE-HEIGHT: 18pt" In very few cases swine and swine products can enter the United States (U.S.). Commercially canned pork is allowed if the CBP officer can determine from the label that the meat was cooked in the can after it was sealed to make it shelf-stable without refrigeration.  p 
 p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN-BOTTOM: 12pt; FONT-SIZE: 12pt; FONT-FAMILY: Gill Sans MT; BACKGROUND: white; LINE-HEIGHT: 18pt"  span style="FONT-WEIGHT: normal; COLOR: #000000" Poultry and poultry products from a country not known to be infected with  a style="TEXT-DECORATION: underline; COLOR: #0000ff" href="https:  www.aphis.usda.gov aphis ourfocus animalhealth nvap NVAP-Reference-Guide Poultry Exotic-Newcastle-disease" Exotic Newcastle Disease  a  (END) and  a style="TEXT-DECORATION: underline; COLOR: #0000ff" href="https:  www.aphis.usda.gov aphis ourfocus animalhealth emergency-management fadprep-hpai" Highly Pathogenic Avian Influenza  a  (HPAI) generally are able to enter the United States.  span style="mso-bidi-font-weight: bold" The product must be unopened and commercially label indicating the country of origin and meat type.  span  Products containing raw egg ingredients are prohibited from most regions. See Animal and Plant Health Inspection Service (APHIS)  span   a style="TEXT-DECORATION: underline" href="http:  www.aphis.usda.gov import_export plants manuals ports downloads apm.pdf" target="_blank"  span style="COLOR: #0000ff" Animal Product  span   span style="COLOR: #0000ff" Import Manual  span   a ,  span style="COLOR: #000000" Chapter 3-6 Eggs and Egg Products.  span   p 
 p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN-BOTTOM: 12pt; FONT-SIZE: 12pt; FONT-FAMILY: Gill Sans MT; COLOR: #000000; LINE-HEIGHT: 18pt; mso-margin-top-alt: auto" As a general rule - if goods are cooked and in shelf-stable (does not require refrigeration) packaging such as cans or other hermetically sealed containers AND they are not from a country affected with various diseases such as Avian influenza, Mad Cow disease, Swine Fever, Exotic Newcastle Disease, etc., they may be admissible.  p 
 p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN-BOTTOM: 12pt; FONT-SIZE: 12pt; FONT-FAMILY: Gill Sans MT; LINE-HEIGHT: 18pt; mso-margin-top-alt: auto"  span style="COLOR: #000000" Please  span style="COLOR: #000000" see  span , Animal Plant Health Inspection Service  span   span style="COLOR: #000000" (APHIS),  span   a style="TEXT-DECORATION: underline" href="https:  www.aphis.usda.gov publications plant_health 2012 fs_imp_food_ppq.pdf"  span style="TEXT-DECORATION: underline; COLOR: #0000ff" Fact Sheet  span   a   span style="COLOR: #000000" on  span   span style="COLOR: #000000" Importing Food and Agricultural Products into the United States.  span   p 
 p style="MARGIN-BOTTOM: 12pt; FONT-SIZE: 12pt; FONT-FAMILY: Gill Sans MT; LINE-HEIGHT: 18pt"  span style="COLOR: #000000" For specific meat products not mentioned above, please reference APHIS's  span   a style="TEXT-DECORATION: underline; COLOR: #0000ff" href="http:  www.aphis.usda.gov import_export plants manuals ports apm.shtml" target="_new" Animal Product Manual  a .  span style="COLOR: #000000" Also, visit the   span  a style="TEXT-DECORATION: underline; COLOR: #0000ff" href="http:  www.usda.gov wps portal usda usdahome" target="_blank" USDA website  a   span style="COLOR: #000000" for the meat and poultry hotline  span .  p 
 p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN-BOTTOM: 12pt; FONT-SIZE: 12pt; FONT-FAMILY: Gill Sans MT; COLOR: #000000; LINE-HEIGHT: 18pt; mso-margin-top-alt: auto" Other than the above general guidelines, it is impossible to advise you in this forum about the admissibility of specific food items because it is so susceptible to change. Disease and pest outbreaks which impact the admissibility status of fresh and packaged food items may occur all over the world at any time.  p 
 p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN-BOTTOM: 12pt; FONT-SIZE: 12pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; LINE-HEIGHT: 18pt; mso-margin-top-alt: auto"  span style="FONT-FAMILY: Gill Sans MT; COLOR: #000000" The USDA establishes criteria for the admissibility of plant, dairy, meat and other animal products returning with travelers and they make the determination for what may be admitted into the U.S.  span   p 
 p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN-BOTTOM: 12pt; FONT-SIZE: 12pt; FONT-FAMILY: Gill Sans MT; LINE-HEIGHT: 18pt; mso-margin-top-alt: auto"  span style="COLOR: #000000" For more information, see APHIS   span  a style="TEXT-DECORATION: underline; COLOR: #0000ff" href="http:  www.aphis.usda.gov import_export index.shtml" target="_new"  span style="TEXT-DECORATION: underline; COLOR: #0000ff" Import  span   span style="TEXT-DECORATION: underline" Export.  span   a   span style="COLOR: #000000" If you have any questions, or require further information related to imports of specific animal products or by-products, please contact the  a style="TEXT-DECORATION: underline; COLOR: #0000ff" href="https:  www.aphis.usda.gov aphis ourfocus animalhealth program-overview ct_ncie" National Center for Import and Export  a  at AskNCIE.Products@aphis.usda.gov  span  span style="COLOR: #000000" . You may also call AHPIS at 301-851-3300 or toll-free at 877-770-5990.  span   p 
 p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN-BOTTOM: 12pt; BACKGROUND: white; LINE-HEIGHT: 18pt"  b style="FONT-SIZE: 12pt; FONT-FAMILY: Gill Sans MT; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; COLOR: #000000" Failure to declare  em  span style="FONT-STYLE: normal" food  span   em  products can result in up to $10,000 in fines and penalties. Even if you believe a  em  span style="FONT-STYLE: normal" food  span   em  item is able to enter the United States you must declare to the officer that you are bringing  em  span style="FONT-STYLE: normal" food  span   em  back. When in doubt keep it out.  b   p 
 p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN-BOTTOM: 12pt; FONT-SIZE: 12pt; FONT-FAMILY: Gill Sans MT; COLOR: #000000; LINE-HEIGHT: 18pt; mso-margin-top-alt: auto" All commercial and personal imports of products from endangered species and other wildlife are subject to  a style="TEXT-DECORATION: underline; COLOR: #0000ff" href="https:  www.fws.gov permits " Fish and Wildlife Service  a  (FWS) permit requirements. For more information, please call the FWS at (703) 358-2093.  p 
 p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN-BOTTOM: 12pt; FONT-SIZE: 12pt; FONT-FAMILY: Gill Sans MT; COLOR: #000000; LINE-HEIGHT: 18pt; mso-margin-top-alt: auto" CBP Agricultural Specialist are stationed at ports of entry and along our land and sea borders. Because of the complexities of regulations governing the importation of food they may need to contact specific agencies for information about what is or is not admissible. If no expert is available, food may be detained in the interest of preventing possible outbreak of foreign diseases in the U.S.  p
