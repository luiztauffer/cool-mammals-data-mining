 p One thing everyone knows about Australia is that it‚Äôs home to some of the most interesting wildlife you‚Äôll ever see. From cuddly and cute to absolutely terrifying, the animals of Australia are certainly fascinating and unique! It‚Äôs one of the very few countries where you‚Äôll find marsupials and egg laying mammals! Below is a list of native Australian animals and some introduced species with a few facts thrown in so you can impress people. This list doesn‚Äôt include all Australian wildlife (yet) but definitely the most well known and a few you‚Äôve probably never heard of as well.  p

 p There are 20 species of bandicoot. They‚Äôre endemic to Australia and they‚Äôre marsupials which are nocturnal and also omnivores (they eat both meat and plants). They‚Äôre also pretty cute.  p 
 p There were 2 species of bilby. The lesser bilby is sadly extinct but the greater bilby can still be found in Australia, although it is endangered.¬†Also known as a rabbit bandicoot, because it‚Äôs very similar to the bandicoot but with very long ears, the bilby is native to Australia.  p 
 p Found in tropical or temperate waters all over the world. If you go near or on the ocean in Australia you‚Äôll be unlucky not to spot bottlenose dolphins at some point during your trip. I highly recommend going on a  a href="https:  nomadsworld.com go-sea-kayak " sea kayaking trip in Byron Bay  a  for a great opportunity to spot dolphins up close.  p 
 p The Brolga used to be known as the ‚ÄúNative Australian Crane‚Äù.¬†As well as being the largest flying bird in Australia it‚Äôs also known for it‚Äôs mating dance. You can find Brolga‚Äôs in many parts of tropical Australia, especially Queensland. In fact it‚Äôs the official bird emblem of Queensland.  p 
 p Camels are not native Australian animals and hopefully that isn‚Äôt too much of a shock to you! They‚Äôre worth a mention simply because there are so many wild camels in the country (around 1.2 million of them!) especially in the Outback. They were imported to help with transportation and construction back in the 19th century but are now feral. There are so many camels that they‚Äôre exported to the Middle East.  p 
 p Also not native to Australia, the cane toad was introduced to Australia in an attempt to control the beetle populations which were destroying sugar cane crops (a major crop for Australia‚Äôs economy). The cane toad spread rapidly through Australia and is also spreading disease. You‚Äôll mainly see these large toads dead on the road or being harassed in quite a few pubs during cane toad races.  p 
 p A terrifying looking bird in many ways but also quite beautiful because of the colours on its head and neck. You can find the cassowary in Northern Queensland although they‚Äôre relatively shy so you might not spot one. Just be aware that even though they‚Äôre shy if you do come across one they‚Äôre dangerous!  p 
 p Cockatoos are fairly common in Australia, they‚Äôre intelligent birds that can be very loud! If you visit the  a href="https:  nomadsworld.com australia arts-factory-lodge " target="_blank" rel="noopener" Arts Factory Lodge  a  book yourself on a free BushTucker Walk to meet Cockatoo Paul and Mr Pickles.  p 
 p The saltie! Saltwater crocodiles are pretty common in waterways in Australia, especially in the north. If you see warning signs telling you to stay out of the water then stay out of the water! Although salties are pretty massive they manage to hide quite well in the water and they‚Äôre quick so you won‚Äôt fare well against one. Of all the Aussie animals listed in this article this one might be the most terrifying!  p

 p Well you‚Äôll have heard of the dingo which, although endangered, can be easily seen on Fraser Island. Dingoes are protected on  a href="https:  nomadsworld.com product 3-day-fraser-island-tour " Fraser Island  a  so if you visit (and you really must) please adhere to all the rules for keeping yourself and the dingoes safe from harm.  p 
 p Dugongs are similar to manatees in appearance and the only marine mammal which is a herbivore (eats only plants). Dugongs are threatened with extinction so if you do see one you‚Äôre very lucky. You‚Äôre more likely to see them in Shark Bay in WA or in Queensland. Dugongs are also known as sea cows or the ‚ÄúLady of The Sea‚Äù and are thought to be the inspiration behind tales of mermaids.  p 
 p Echidna‚Äôs really are one of the oddest animals of Australia. One of only 2 egg laying mammals that exist in the whole world, it looks a lot like a porcupine but is more closely related to a platypus. After laying a single egg 22 days after mating, the female echidna deposits the egg directly into her pouch where it remains for up to 2 months. Baby echidnas are called puggles which is just the best name for a baby animal! Male echidnas have a four headed penis! Moving on‚Ä¶  p 
 p Large, terrifying up close and flightless, emu‚Äôs are not one of the most popular animals of Australia. Surprising then that they appear on the Australian Coat of Arms alongside the kangaroo. Possibly even more surprising is that they were allowed to remain on the Coat of Arms after they beat the Australian army in the unbelievable, but very real,¬† a href="https:  nomadsworld.com great-emu-war " Great Emu War  a . Emu‚Äôs are endemic to Australia and are it‚Äôs largest bird.  p

 p Even 10 years after first seeing penguins in Australia with my very own eyes it still seems strange to me that what is thought of as a very hot country would have an animal that is most well known for living in icy climates. But there we go. Fairy penguins, which are incredibly cute, are quite commonly seen in Australia, most famously on Phillip Island where you can witness for yourself their  a href="https:  nomadsworld.com phillip-island-penguin-parade-day-trip " parade  a  up the beach. I highly recommend doing a tour to see them but please DON‚ÄôT take pictures using your flash!  p 
 p Just to be confusing this is a bat not a fox. The grey headed flying fox is a mega-bat endemic to Australia. It‚Äôs mostly found in the south eastern parts of the country. The little red flying fox is also a native mega-bat but living in north and eastern areas. It‚Äôs the smallest of Australia‚Äôs flying foxes. Also in the north eastern parts of the country you‚Äôll find the spectacled flying fox (or spectacled fruit bat). Finally you may also see a black flying fox on your travels. A mega bat is larger than usual bats, they‚Äôre also known as fruit bats and many do not use echo-location.  p 
 p Frill necked lizards are fairly hard to spot as their colours help them to stay camouflaged, except when they display their colourful frills of course. These lizards are relatively large but harmless and if you keep your eyes open you might spot one in the northern territories.  p 
 p The Australian fur seal is the largest of all fur seals and the 4th rarest species of seal in the world, so they‚Äôre a protected animal in Australia. Fur seals tend to be found in the south of the country with Seal Rock on Phillip Island being a good place to look for them!  p 
 p A galah is not just an  a href="https:  nomadsworld.com aussie-slang " Australian slang  a  word for a stupid person, it‚Äôs also¬†an Australian cockatoo with a reputation for not being too bright.  p 
 p If you plan on doing some underwater exploration of the Great Barrier Reef during your trip to Australia then you‚Äôre quite likely to come across a giant clam or two. Giant clams can weigh up to a whopping 200kg and can live for 100 years. They start their lives male but become hermaphrodites (can produce both sperm and eggs).  p 
 p A Goanna is a type of monitor lizard which varies in size from just 20cm long to 2 metres! Depending on what source you use there are thought to be either 20 or 30 species of monitor lizard, 15 or 25 of which can be found in Australia.  p 
 p The only reason I didn‚Äôt list this as the most terrifying on this list of Australian animals is because Great Whites have had a bit of a raw deal. Yes, they‚Äôre scary and yes they can be deadly but the majority of the time they‚Äôre not going to bother you. The Jaws films have a lot to answer for with regard to this animals reputation. They can grow up to 6 metres long and are scary looking but my advice? Don‚Äôt go in the water during a sharks dinner time!  p

 p Alongside the koala, kangaroos are probably the most well known of all the animals of Australia. The red kangaroo is the largest while the grey kangaroo is the one you‚Äôre likely to see most often.
