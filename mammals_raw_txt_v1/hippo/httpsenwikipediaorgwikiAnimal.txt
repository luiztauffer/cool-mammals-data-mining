 p  b Animals  b  are  a href=" wiki Multicellular" class="mw-redirect" title="Multicellular" multicellular  a   a href=" wiki Eukaryotic" class="mw-redirect" title="Eukaryotic" eukaryotic  a  organisms that form the  a href=" wiki Kingdom_(biology)" title="Kingdom (biology)" biological kingdom  a   b Animalia  b . With few exceptions, animals  a href=" wiki Heterotroph" title="Heterotroph" consume organic material  a ,  a href=" wiki Cellular_respiration#Aerobic_respiration" title="Cellular respiration" breathe oxygen  a , are  a href=" wiki Motility" title="Motility" able to move  a , can  a href=" wiki Sexual_reproduction" title="Sexual reproduction" reproduce sexually  a , and grow from a hollow sphere of cells, the  a href=" wiki Blastula" title="Blastula" blastula  a , during  a href=" wiki Embryogenesis" class="mw-redirect" title="Embryogenesis" embryonic development  a . Over 1.5 million  a href=" wiki Extant_taxon" class="mw-redirect" title="Extant taxon" living  a  animal  a href=" wiki Species" title="Species" species  a  have been  a href=" wiki Species_description" title="Species description" described  a —of which around 1 million are  a href=" wiki Insecta" class="mw-redirect" title="Insecta" insects  a —but it has been estimated there are over 7 million animal species in total. Animals range in length from 8.5 millionths of a metre to 33.6 metres (110 ft) and have  a href=" wiki Ecology" title="Ecology" complex interactions  a  with each other and their environments, forming intricate  a href=" wiki Food_web" title="Food web" food webs  a . The category includes  a href=" wiki Human" title="Human" humans  a , but in colloquial use the term  i animal  i  often refers only to non-human animals. The study of non-human animals is known as  a href=" wiki Zoology" title="Zoology" zoology  a .
  p 
 p Most living animal species are in the  a href=" wiki Bilateria" title="Bilateria" Bilateria  a , a  a href=" wiki Clade" title="Clade" clade  a  whose members have a  a href=" wiki Symmetry_in_biology#Bilateral_symmetry" title="Symmetry in biology" bilaterally symmetric  a  body plan. The Bilateria include the  a href=" wiki Protostomes" class="mw-redirect" title="Protostomes" protostomes  a —in which many groups of  a href=" wiki Invertebrate" title="Invertebrate" invertebrates  a  are found, such as  a href=" wiki Nematode" title="Nematode" nematodes  a ,  a href=" wiki Arthropod" title="Arthropod" arthropods  a , and  a href=" wiki Mollusc" class="mw-redirect" title="Mollusc" molluscs  a —and the  a href=" wiki Deuterostomes" class="mw-redirect" title="Deuterostomes" deuterostomes  a , containing the  a href=" wiki Echinoderm" title="Echinoderm" echinoderms  a  and  a href=" wiki Chordate" title="Chordate" chordates  a  (including the  a href=" wiki Vertebrate" title="Vertebrate" vertebrates  a ). Life forms interpreted as early animals were present in the  a href=" wiki Ediacaran_biota" title="Ediacaran biota" Ediacaran biota  a  of the late  a href=" wiki Precambrian" title="Precambrian" Precambrian  a . Many modern animal phyla became clearly established in the  a href=" wiki Fossil_record" class="mw-redirect" title="Fossil record" fossil record  a  as  a href=" wiki Marine_life" title="Marine life" marine species  a  during the  a href=" wiki Cambrian_explosion" title="Cambrian explosion" Cambrian explosion  a  which began around 542 million years ago. 6,331 groups of  a href=" wiki Gene" title="Gene" genes  a  common to all living animals have been identified; these may have arisen from a single  a href=" wiki Animal#Phylogeny" title="Animal" common ancestor  a  that lived  a href=" wiki Cryogenian" title="Cryogenian" 650 million years ago  a .
  p 
 p  a href=" wiki Aristotle%27s_biology" title="Aristotle's biology" Aristotle divided animals  a  into those with blood and those without.  a href=" wiki Carl_Linnaeus" title="Carl Linnaeus" Carl Linnaeus  a  created the first hierarchical  a href=" wiki Taxonomy_(biology)" title="Taxonomy (biology)" biological classification  a  for animals in 1758 with his  i  a href=" wiki Systema_Naturae" title="Systema Naturae" Systema Naturae  a   i , which  a href=" wiki Jean-Baptiste_Lamarck" title="Jean-Baptiste Lamarck" Jean-Baptiste Lamarck  a  expanded into 14  a href=" wiki Phylum" title="Phylum" phyla  a  by 1809. In 1874,  a href=" wiki Ernst_Haeckel" title="Ernst Haeckel" Ernst Haeckel  a  divided the animal kingdom into the multicellular  b Metazoa  b  (now  a href=" wiki Synonym_(taxonomy)" title="Synonym (taxonomy)" synonymous  a  with Animalia) and the  a href=" wiki Protozoa" title="Protozoa" Protozoa  a , single-celled organisms no longer considered animals. In modern times, the biological classification of animals relies on advanced techniques, such as  a href=" wiki Molecular_phylogenetics" title="Molecular phylogenetics" molecular phylogenetics  a , which are effective at demonstrating the evolutionary relationships between animal  a href=" wiki Taxon" title="Taxon" taxa  a .
  p 
 p Humans make use of many other animal species for food, including  a href=" wiki Meat" title="Meat" meat  a ,  a href=" wiki Milk" title="Milk" milk  a , and  a href=" wiki Egg" title="Egg" eggs  a ; for materials, such as  a href=" wiki Leather" title="Leather" leather  a  and  a href=" wiki Wool" title="Wool" wool  a ; as  a href=" wiki Pet" title="Pet" pets  a ; and as  a href=" wiki Working_animal" title="Working animal" working animals  a  for power and transport. Dogs have been  a href=" wiki Hunting_dog" title="Hunting dog" used in hunting  a , while many terrestrial and aquatic animals are hunted for sport. Non-human animals have appeared in art from the earliest times and are featured in mythology and religion.

 p Animals have several characteristics that set them apart from other living things. These fossils are interpreted as most probably being early  a href=" wiki Porifera" class="mw-redirect" title="Porifera" sponges  a . sup id="cite_ref-roseMaloof_80-0" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-roseMaloof-80" [78]  a   sup

 p The oldest animals are found in the  a href=" wiki Ediacaran_biota" title="Ediacaran biota" Ediacaran biota  a , towards the end of the Precambrian, around 610 million years ago. It had long been doubtful whether these included animals, sup id="cite_ref-81" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-81" [79]  a   sup  sup id="cite_ref-82" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-82" [80]  a   sup  sup id="cite_ref-83" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-83" [81]  a   sup  but the discovery of the animal lipid  a href=" wiki Cholesterol" title="Cholesterol" cholesterol  a  in fossils of  i  a href=" wiki Dickinsonia" title="Dickinsonia" Dickinsonia  a   i  establishes that these were indeed animals. sup id="cite_ref-Bobrovskiy_Hope_Ivantsov_Nettersheim_pp._1246–1249_79-1" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-Bobrovskiy_Hope_Ivantsov_Nettersheim_pp._1246%E2%80%931249-79" [77]  a   sup

The apparent suddenness of the event may however be an artefact of the fossil record, rather than showing that all these animals appeared simultaneously. sup id="cite_ref-84" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-84" [82]  a   sup  sup id="cite_ref-85" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-85" [83]  a   sup  sup id="cite_ref-86" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-86" [84]  a   sup  sup id="cite_ref-Royal_Ontario_Museum_87-0" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-Royal_Ontario_Museum-87" [85]  a   sup

 p Several animal phyla lack bilateral symmetry. Among these, the sponges (Porifera) probably diverged first, representing the oldest animal phylum. sup id="cite_ref-106" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-106" [104]  a   sup  Sponges lack the complex organization found in most other animal phyla; sup id="cite_ref-107" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-107" [105]  a   sup  their cells are differentiated, but in most cases not organised into distinct tissues. sup id="cite_ref-108" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-108" [106]  a   sup  They typically feed by drawing in water through pores. sup id="cite_ref-109" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-109" [107]  a   sup 
  p 
 p The Ctenophora (comb jellies) and Cnidaria (which includes  a href=" wiki Jellyfish" title="Jellyfish" jellyfish  a ,  a href=" wiki Sea_anemone" title="Sea anemone" sea anemones  a , and corals) are radially symmetric and have digestive chambers with a single opening, which serves as both mouth and anus. sup id="cite_ref-110" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-110" [108]  a   sup  Animals in both phyla have distinct tissues, but these are not organised into  a href=" wiki Organ_(anatomy)" title="Organ (anatomy)" organs  a . sup id="cite_ref-111" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-111" [109]  a   sup  They are  a href=" wiki Diploblastic" class="mw-redirect" title="Diploblastic" diploblastic  a , having only two main germ layers, ectoderm and endoderm. sup id="cite_ref-112" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-112" [110]  a   sup  The tiny  a href=" wiki Placozoan" class="mw-redirect" title="Placozoan" placozoans  a  are similar, but they do not have a permanent digestive chamber. sup id="cite_ref-113" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-113" [111]  a   sup  sup id="cite_ref-114" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-114" [112]  a   sup 
  p 
 p The remaining animals, the great majority—comprising some 29 phyla and over a million species—form a clade, the Bilateria. The body is  a href=" wiki Triploblasty" title="Triploblasty" triploblastic  a , with three well-developed germ layers, and their tissues  a href=" wiki Organogenesis" title="Organogenesis" form distinct organs  a . The digestive chamber has two openings, a mouth and an anus, and there is an internal body cavity, a  a href=" wiki Coelom" title="Coelom" coelom  a  or pseudocoelom. Animals with this bilaterally symmetric  a href=" wiki Body_plan" title="Body plan" body plan  a  and a tendency to move in one direction have a head end (anterior) and a tail end (posterior) as well as a back (dorsal) and a belly (ventral); therefore they also have a left side and a right side. sup id="cite_ref-Minelli2009_116-0" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-Minelli2009-116" [113]  a   sup  sup id="cite_ref-Brusca2016_117-0" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-Brusca2016-117" [114]  a   sup 
  p 
 p Having a front end means that this part of the body encounters stimuli, such as food, favouring  a href=" wiki Cephalisation" class="mw-redirect" title="Cephalisation" cephalisation  a , the development of a head with  a href=" wiki Sense_organ" class="mw-redirect" title="Sense organ" sense organs  a  and a mouth. Many bilaterians have a combination of circular  a href=" wiki Muscle" title="Muscle" muscles  a  that constrict the body, making it longer, and an opposing set of longitudinal muscles, that shorten the body; sup id="cite_ref-Brusca2016_117-1" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-Brusca2016-117" [114]  a   sup  these enable soft-bodied animals with a  a href=" wiki Hydrostatic_skeleton" title="Hydrostatic skeleton" hydrostatic skeleton  a  to move by  a href=" wiki Peristalsis" title="Peristalsis" peristalsis  a . sup id="cite_ref-Quillin_118-0" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-Quillin-118" [115]  a   sup  They also have a gut that extends through the basically cylindrical body from mouth to anus. Many bilaterian phyla have primary  a href=" wiki Larva" title="Larva" larvae  a  which swim with  a href=" wiki Cilia" class="mw-redirect" title="Cilia" cilia  a  and have an apical organ containing sensory cells. However, there are exceptions to each of these characteristics; for example, adult echinoderms are radially symmetric (unlike their larvae), while some  a href=" wiki Helminths" class="mw-redirect" title="Helminths" parasitic worms  a  have extremely simplified body structures. sup id="cite_ref-Minelli2009_116-1" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-Minelli2009-116" [113]  a   sup  sup id="cite_ref-Brusca2016_117-2" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-Brusca2016-117" [114]  a   sup

 p Genetic studies have considerably changed zoologists' understanding of the relationships within the Bilateria.  p Protostomes and deuterostomes differ in several ways. Early in development, deuterostome embryos undergo radial  a href=" wiki Cleavage_(embryo)" title="Cleavage (embryo)" cleavage  a  during cell division, while many protostomes (the  a href=" wiki Spiralia" title="Spiralia" Spiralia  a ) undergo spiral cleavage. sup id="cite_ref-123" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-123" [120]  a   sup

In deuterostomes, the anus forms first while the mouth develops secondarily. sup id="cite_ref-124" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-124" [121]  a   sup  sup id="cite_ref-Hejnol2009_125-0" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-Hejnol2009-125" [122]  a   sup  Most protostomes have  a href=" wiki Schizocoely" title="Schizocoely" schizocoelous development  a , where cells simply fill in the interior of the gastrula to form the mesoderm.
