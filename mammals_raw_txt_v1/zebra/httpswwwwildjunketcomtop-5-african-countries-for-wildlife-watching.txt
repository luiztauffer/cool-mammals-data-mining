 p align="justify" As a huge fan of wildlife watching, I’ve spied on a leopard up close in the  a href="https:  www.wildjunket.com 2008 07 05 those-travelling-days " target="_blank" rel="noopener" Serengeti National Park  a , observed sea lions on the  a href="https:  www.wildjunket.com 2009 10 13 how-to-see-the-galapagos-islands-on-the-cheap " target="_blank" rel="noopener" Galapagos Islands  a  and tip-toed around a snoozing koala bear in  a href="https:  www.wildjunket.com 2009 03 09 melbourne-australia-a-city-of-different-faces " target="_blank" rel="noopener" Melbourne, Australia  a . Being able to observe wildlife in a natural setting is a rare, unique and one-in-a-million experience.  p 
 p align="justify" Out of the numerous wildlife watching spots in the world, African countries dominate the list with their awe-inspiring savannahs and untouched surroundings. I bring you five of the best in Africa, based on my own experience and readings.  p 
 p align="justify"  a href="https:  www.wildjunket.com category by-country africa botswana-africa " Botswana  a  stands out for its diverse ecosystems: on a wildlife safari in Botswana, you’re never limited to just one transport mode. Besides game-tracking by 4WD, you’ll be exploring Botswana’s mixture of salt pans and savannahs by mokoro (dugout canoe, Botswana specialty) and riverboat. Places not to miss include the  a href="https:  www.wildjunket.com 2012 08 28 chobe-river "  strong Chobe National Park  strong   a , home to the world’s largest herds of elephant, and the  a href="https:  www.wildjunket.com 2011 12 19 video-floating-in-the-okavango-delta-botswana "  strong Okavango Delta  strong   a , an exquisite wetland area. Botswana lodges are also world class, often found in the heart of African wilderness.  p 
 p align="justify" A country bursting with unspoiled beauty,  a href="https:  www.wildjunket.com category by-country africa tanzania " target="_blank" rel="noopener" Tanzania  a  has some of Africa’s most coveted wildlife watching spots. Amongst them, my personal favorite is  strong Ngorongoro Crater  strong . At daybreak, as the mist dissipates, you’ll see silhouettes of elephants, rhinoceros, lions and buffalos appearing before you. The long drive through Serengeti National Park is an epic one, as you get to witness the massive wildebeest migration. Whether you’re looking for a luxury safari or a basic camping safari, a trip to the wildlife epicenter of Africa is an essential part of your trip to Tanzania.  p 
 p align="justify"  span style="color: #333333;" What’s unique about  a href="https:  www.wildjunket.com category by-country africa namibia " Namibia  a  is its expansive deserts, red sand dunes and a kaleidoscope of different environments. Straddling two great deserts: the Namib, the oldest desert in the world, and the red sand dunes along the Atlantic Coastline. Some of the best wildlife-watching spots include the white saltpans of  a href="https:  www.wildjunket.com 2012 02 06 photoblog-wildlife-encounters-in-etosha-national-park-namibia "  strong Etosha National Park  strong    a (herds of giraffes), red dunes at  a href="https:  www.wildjunket.com 2011 12 13 photoblog-watching-sunrise-atop-sand-dunes-in-sossusvlei-namibia "  strong Sossusvlei   strong   a (to see gemsbok) and  strong Skeleton Coast  strong  (colonies of seals).  span   p 
 p align="justify" Rwanda is best known for its excellent  a href="https:  www.wildjunket.com 2013 11 06 up-close-and-personal-with-the-silverback-gorilla-tracking-in-uganda-part-i " gorilla tracking opportunities  a . Unlike most of the other countries on this list, gorillas are Rwanda’s star celebrity in the wildlife world. The  strong Volcanoes National Park  strong  , in northern Rwanda is home to the world’s largest number of endangered mountain gorillas. In Eastern Rwanda, the  strong Akagera National Park  strong  in eastern Rwanda is crowded with wildlife – from herds of elephant and buffaloes to giraffes and zebras grazing its savannahs and a dozen species of antelope roaming free.  p 
 p align="justify"  a href="https:  www.wildjunket.com category by-country africa south-africa " South Africa  a  has an edge over the other countries, and it’s that besides wildlife safaris, its cities have even more to offer: cultural activities, ethnical history and cosmopolitan city life.  strong Kruger National Park  strong , its largest and most diverse game reserve, is home to all of Africa’s Big Five. Several other  a href="https:  www.wildjunket.com 2011 11 21 5-national-parks-in-south-africa-worth-visiting " national parks in South Africa  a  are also said to be malaria-free, namely KwaZulu Natal, Madikwe Game Reserve and the Waterberg. South Africa is lauded as the #1 best place to go on an  a href="https:  www.wildjunket.com african-safari-with-kids-complete-guide " African safari with kids  a  as it’s home to lots of family-friendly lodges that are also malaria-free.  p
