 p The  b giraffe  b  ( i Giraffa  i ) is a  a href=" wiki Genus" title="Genus" genus  a  of African  a href=" wiki Even-toed_ungulate" title="Even-toed ungulate" even-toed ungulate  a   a href=" wiki Mammal" title="Mammal" mammals  a , the  a href=" wiki Largest_mammals#Even-toed_Ungulates_(Artiodactyla)" class="mw-redirect" title="Largest mammals" tallest  a  living terrestrial animals and the largest  a href=" wiki Ruminant" title="Ruminant" ruminants  a . Taxonomic classifications of one to eight extant giraffe species have been described, based upon research into the  a href=" wiki Mitochondrial_DNA" title="Mitochondrial DNA" mitochondrial  a  and  a href=" wiki Nuclear_DNA" title="Nuclear DNA" nuclear DNA  a , as well as morphological measurements of  i Giraffa,  i  but the  a href=" wiki International_Union_for_Conservation_of_Nature" title="International Union for Conservation of Nature" International Union for Conservation of Nature  a  currently recognises only one species,  i Giraffa camelopardalis  i , the  a href=" wiki Type_species" title="Type species" type species  a , with nine subspecies. Seven other species are extinct, prehistoric species known from fossils. 
  p 
 p The giraffe's chief distinguishing characteristics are its extremely long neck and legs, its horn-like  a href=" wiki Ossicone" title="Ossicone" ossicones  a , and its distinctive coat patterns. It is classified under the  a href=" wiki Family_(biology)" title="Family (biology)" family  a   a href=" wiki Giraffidae" title="Giraffidae" Giraffidae  a , along with its closest extant relative, the  a href=" wiki Okapi" title="Okapi" okapi  a . Its scattered range extends from  a href=" wiki Chad" title="Chad" Chad  a  in the north to  a href=" wiki South_Africa" title="South Africa" South Africa  a  in the south, and from  a href=" wiki Niger" title="Niger" Niger  a  in the west to  a href=" wiki Somalia" title="Somalia" Somalia  a  in the east. Giraffes usually inhabit  a href=" wiki Savannah" class="mw-redirect" title="Savannah" savannahs  a  and  a href=" wiki Woodland" title="Woodland" woodlands  a . Their food source is leaves, fruits and flowers of woody plants, primarily  a href=" wiki Acacieae" class="mw-redirect" title="Acacieae" acacia  a  species, which they browse at heights most other herbivores cannot reach. They may be preyed on by  a href=" wiki Lion" title="Lion" lions  a ,  a href=" wiki Leopard" title="Leopard" leopards  a ,  a href=" wiki Spotted_hyena" title="Spotted hyena" spotted hyenas  a  and  a href=" wiki African_wild_dog" title="African wild dog" African wild dogs  a . Giraffes live in herds of related females and their offspring, or bachelor herds of unrelated adult males, but are gregarious and may gather in large aggregations. Males establish social hierarchies through "necking", which are combat bouts where the neck is used as a weapon.  a href=" wiki Dominance_(ethology)" title="Dominance (ethology)" Dominant  a  males gain mating access to females, which bear the sole responsibility for raising the young.
  p 
 p The giraffe has intrigued various cultures, both ancient and modern, for its peculiar appearance, and has often been featured in paintings, books, and cartoons. It is classified by the  a href=" wiki International_Union_for_Conservation_of_Nature" title="International Union for Conservation of Nature" International Union for Conservation of Nature  a  as  a href=" wiki Vulnerable_species" title="Vulnerable species" Vulnerable  a  to extinction, and has been  a href=" wiki Local_extinction" title="Local extinction" extirpated  a  from many parts of its former range. Giraffes are still found in numerous  a href=" wiki National_park" title="National park" national parks  a  and  a href=" wiki Game_reserve" title="Game reserve" game reserves  a  but estimations as of 2016 indicate that there are approximately 97,500 members of  i Giraffa  i  in the wild. More than 1,600 were kept in zoos in 2010.

 p The giraffe is one of only two living genera of the family  a href=" wiki Giraffidae" title="Giraffidae" Giraffidae  a  in the order  a href=" wiki Artiodactyla" class="mw-redirect" title="Artiodactyla" Artiodactyla  a , the other being the  a href=" wiki Okapi" title="Okapi" okapi  a . The family was once much more extensive, with over 10 fossil  a href=" wiki Genus" title="Genus" genera  a  described. Their closest known relatives are the extinct deer-like  a href=" wiki Climacoceratidae" title="Climacoceratidae" climacocerids  a . They, together with the family  a href=" wiki Antilocapridae" title="Antilocapridae" Antilocapridae  a  (whose only extant species is the  a href=" wiki Pronghorn" title="Pronghorn" pronghorn  a ), belong to the superfamily  a href=" wiki Giraffoidea" title="Giraffoidea" Giraffoidea  a . These animals may have evolved from the extinct family  a href=" wiki Palaeomerycidae" title="Palaeomerycidae" Palaeomerycidae  a  which might also have been the ancestor of  a href=" wiki Deer" title="Deer" deer  a . sup id="cite_ref-Mitchell20003_11-0" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-Mitchell20003-11" [11]  a   sup

 p The elongation of the neck appears to have started early in the giraffe  a href=" wiki Lineage_(evolution)" title="Lineage (evolution)" lineage  a . Comparisons between giraffes and their ancient relatives suggest that vertebrae close to the skull lengthened earlier, followed by lengthening of vertebrae further down. sup id="cite_ref-Danowitz2015_12-0" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-Danowitz2015-12" [12]  a   sup  One early giraffid ancestor was  i  a href=" wiki Canthumeryx" title="Canthumeryx" Canthumeryx  a   i  which has been dated variously to have lived 25–20 million years ago (mya), 17–15 mya or 18–14.3 mya and whose deposits have been found in Libya. This animal was medium-sized, slender and antelope-like.  i  a href=" wiki Giraffokeryx" title="Giraffokeryx" Giraffokeryx  a   i  appeared 15 mya in the  a href=" wiki Indian_subcontinent" title="Indian subcontinent" Indian subcontinent  a  and resembled an okapi or a small giraffe, and had a longer neck and similar  a href=" wiki Ossicones" class="mw-redirect" title="Ossicones" ossicones  a . sup id="cite_ref-Mitchell20003_11-1" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-Mitchell20003-11" [11]  a   sup   i Giraffokeryx  i  may have shared a clade with more massively built giraffids like  i  a href=" wiki Sivatherium" title="Sivatherium" Sivatherium  a   i  and  i  a href=" wiki Bramatherium" title="Bramatherium" Bramatherium  a   i . sup id="cite_ref-Danowitz2015_12-1" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-Danowitz2015-12" [12]  a   sup

 p Giraffids like  i  a href=" wiki Palaeotragus" title="Palaeotragus" Palaeotragus  a   i ,  i  a href=" wiki Shansitherium" title="Shansitherium" Shansitherium  a   i  and  i  a href=" wiki Samotherium" title="Samotherium" Samotherium  a   i  appeared 14 mya and lived throughout Africa and Eurasia. These animals had bare ossicones and small cranial sinuses and were longer with broader skulls. sup id="cite_ref-Mitchell20003_11-2" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-Mitchell20003-11" [11]  a   sup  sup id="cite_ref-Danowitz2015_12-2" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-Danowitz2015-12" [12]  a   sup   i Paleotragus  i  resembled the okapi and may have been its ancestor. sup id="cite_ref-Mitchell20003_11-3" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-Mitchell20003-11" [11]  a   sup  Others find that the okapi lineage diverged earlier, before  i Giraffokeryx  i . sup id="cite_ref-Danowitz2015_12-3" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-Danowitz2015-12" [12]  a   sup    i Samotherium  i  was a particularly important  a href=" wiki Transitional_fossil" title="Transitional fossil" transitional fossil  a  in the giraffe lineage as its cervical vertebrae was intermediate in length and structure between a modern giraffe and an okapi, and was more vertical than the okapi's. sup id="cite_ref-Danowitz_13-1" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-Danowitz-13" [13]  a   sup   i  a href=" wiki Bohlinia" title="Bohlinia" Bohlinia  a   i , which first appeared in southeastern Europe and lived 9–7 mya was likely a direct ancestor of the giraffe.  i Bohlinia  i  closely resembled modern giraffes, having a long neck and legs and similar ossicones and dentition. sup id="cite_ref-Mitchell20003_11-4" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-Mitchell20003-11" [11]  a   sup

 p  i Bohlinia  i  entered China and northern India in response to climate change. From there, the genus  i  a href=" wiki Giraffa" class="mw-redirect" title="Giraffa" Giraffa  a   i  evolved and, around 7 mya, entered Africa. sup id="cite_ref-TPBDBGiraffa_14-0" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-TPBDBGiraffa-14" [14]  a   sup  Further climate changes caused the extinction of the Asian giraffes, while the African giraffes survived and radiated into several new species. Living giraffes appear to have arisen around 1 mya in eastern Africa during the  a href=" wiki Pleistocene" title="Pleistocene" Pleistocene  a . sup id="cite_ref-Mitchell20003_11-5" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-Mitchell20003-11" [11]  a   sup  Some biologists suggest the modern giraffes descended from  i  a href=" wiki Giraffa_jumae" title="Giraffa jumae" G. jumae  a   i ; sup id="cite_ref-sim1996_15-0" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-sim1996-15" [15]  a   sup  others find  i  a href=" w index.php?title=Giraffa_gracilis&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Giraffa gracilis (page does not exist)" G. gracilis  a   i  a more likely candidate. sup id="cite_ref-Mitchell20003_11-6" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-Mitchell20003-11" [11]  a   sup   i G. jumae  i  was larger and more heavily built while  i G. gracilis  i  was smaller and more lightly built. The main driver for the evolution of the giraffes is believed to have been the changes from extensive forests to more open habitats, which began 8 mya. sup id="cite_ref-Mitchell20003_11-7" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-Mitchell20003-11" [11]  a   sup  During this time, tropical plants disappeared and were replaced by arid  a href=" wiki C4_plant" class="mw-redirect" title="C4 plant" C4 plants  a , and a dry savannah emerged across eastern and northern Africa and western India. sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-16" [16]  a   sup  sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-17" [17]  a   sup  Some researchers have hypothesised that this new habitat coupled with a different diet, including acacia species, may have exposed giraffe ancestors to toxins that caused higher mutation rates and a higher rate of evolution. sup id="cite_ref-bada_18-0" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-bada-18" [18]  a   sup  The coat patterns of modern giraffes may also have coincided with these habitat changes. Asian giraffes are hypothesised to have had more okapi-like colourations. sup id="cite_ref-Mitchell20003_11-8" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-Mitchell20003-11" [11]  a   sup

 p In the early 19th century,  a href=" wiki Jean-Baptiste_Lamarck" title="Jean-Baptiste Lamarck" Jean-Baptiste Lamarck  a  believed the giraffe's long neck was an "acquired characteristic", developed as generations of ancestral giraffes strove to reach the leaves of tall trees. sup id="cite_ref-Prothero_2003_19-0" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-Prothero_2003-19" [19]  a   sup  This theory was eventually rejected, and scientists now believe the giraffe's neck arose through Darwinian  a href=" wiki Natural_selection" title="Natural selection" natural selection  a —that ancestral giraffes with long necks thereby had a competitive feeding advantage (competing browsers hypothesis) sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-20" [20]  a   sup  that better enabled them to survive and reproduce to pass on their genes. sup id="cite_ref-Prothero_2003_19-1" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-Prothero_2003-19" [19]  a   sup

 p The giraffe  a href=" wiki Genome" title="Genome" genome  a  is around 2.9 billion  a href=" wiki Base_pair" title="Base pair" base pairs  a  in length compared to the 3.3 billion base pairs of the okapi. Of the proteins in giraffe and okapi genes, 19.4% are identical. The two species are equally distantly related to cattle, suggesting the giraffe's unique characteristics are not because of faster evolution. The  a href=" wiki Divergent_evolution" title="Divergent evolution" divergence  a  of giraffe and okapi lineages dates to around 11.5 mya. A small group of  a href=" wiki Regulatory_gene" class="mw-redirect" title="Regulatory gene" regulatory genes  a  in the giraffe appear to be responsible for the animal's stature and associated circulatory adaptations. sup id="cite_ref-Genome_21-0" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-Genome-21" [21]  a   sup

 p The IUCN currently recognises only one species of giraffe with nine subspecies. sup id="cite_ref-:1_23-0" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-:1-23" [23]  a   sup  sup id="cite_ref-:3_24-0" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-:3-24" [24]  a   sup  In 2001, a two-species taxonomy was proposed. sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-25" [25]  a   sup  A 2007 study on the genetics of  i Giraffa  i , suggested they were six species: the West African, Rothschild's, reticulated, Masai, Angolan, and South African giraffe. sup id="cite_ref-GeneticStructure_22-1" class="reference"  a href="#cite_note-GeneticStructure-22" [22]  a   sup  The study deduced from genetic differences in  a href=" wiki Nuclear_DNA" title="Nuclear DNA" nuclear  a  and  a href=" wiki Mitochondrial_DNA" title="Mitochondrial DNA" mitochondrial DNA  a  (mtDNA) that giraffes from these populations are  a href=" wiki Reproductive_isolation" title="Reproductive isolation" reproductively isolated  a  and rarely interbreed, though no natural obstacles block their mutual access.  p Reticulated and Masai giraffes have the highest mtDNA diversity, which is consistent with giraffes originating in eastern Africa.
