 p The  b Sichuan Giant Panda Sanctuaries  b  ( a href=" wiki Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters" simplified Chinese  a :  span lang="zh-Hans" 四川大熊猫栖息地  span ;  a href=" wiki Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters" traditional Chinese  a :  span lang="zh-Hant" 四川大熊貓棲息地  span ;  a href=" wiki Pinyin" title="Pinyin" pinyin  a :  i  span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" Sìchuān Dàxióngmāo Qīxīdì  span   i ) located in southwest  a href=" wiki Sichuan" title="Sichuan" Sichuan  a  province of  a href=" wiki China" title="China" China  a , is the home to more than 30% of the world's highly endangered  a href=" wiki Giant_panda" title="Giant panda" giant pandas  a  and is among the most important sites for the captive breeding of these pandas. It covers 9245 km sup 2  sup  with seven nature reserves and nine scenic parks in the  a href=" wiki Qionglai_Mountains" title="Qionglai Mountains" Qionglai  a  and Jiajin Mountains. Along with the giant panda, the sanctuary is a refuge to other endangered species such as the  a href=" wiki Red_panda" title="Red panda" red panda  a , the  a href=" wiki Snow_leopard" title="Snow leopard" snow leopard  a , and the  a href=" wiki Clouded_leopard" title="Clouded leopard" clouded leopard  a . Outside of the tropical  a href=" wiki Rainforest" title="Rainforest" rainforests  a , it is among the botanically richest sites of  the world, and is home to between 5,000 and 6,000 species of flora. It has been noted that the region is similar to the paleo-tropic forests of the  a href=" wiki Tertiary" title="Tertiary" Tertiary Era  a .
