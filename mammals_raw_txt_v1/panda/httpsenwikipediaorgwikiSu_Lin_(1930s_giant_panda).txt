 p Harkness returned to America with the bottle-fed cub, and Su Lin became the first live panda to be displayed in the United States.  In April 1937, the panda was purchased by  a href=" wiki Brookfield_Zoo" title="Brookfield Zoo" Brookfield Zoo  a  outside of  a href=" wiki Chicago" title="Chicago" Chicago  a , where he was visited by such celebrities as  a href=" wiki Shirley_Temple" title="Shirley Temple" Shirley Temple  a ,  a href=" wiki Kermit_Roosevelt" title="Kermit Roosevelt" Kermit Roosevelt  a , and  a href=" wiki Helen_Hayes" title="Helen Hayes" Helen Hayes  a . Harkness brought a second panda, Mei-Mei, to be a companion for Su Lin at the zoo in February 1938. However, the two animals fought with each other, and were soon separated.
